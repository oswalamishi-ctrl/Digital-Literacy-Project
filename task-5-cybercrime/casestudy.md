Cybercrime Case Study: 
UPI Payment Fraud 

UPI payment fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in 
India. In this type of fraud, cybercriminals trick users into sharing 
sensitive information or approving fake payment requests. For 
example, a college student received a call from a person claiming 
to be from a bank. The caller informed the student that their bank 
account would be blocked unless they verified their details. The 
caller then sent a payment request and asked the student to 
approve it. 

The student believed the caller and approved the request. Within 
seconds, money was deducted from their bank account. The 
student later realized that they had been scammed. In many 
cases, fraudsters also send fake links, QR codes, or ask users to 
share OTPs. 
Students and new digital users are often targeted because they 
may not be aware of such scams. The consequences include 
financial loss, stress, and sometimes identity theft. This case 
highlights the importance of digital awareness and careful 
handling of online transactions. Users should always verify calls 
and avoid sharing personal banking details.
